What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, servers, networks, and digital data from cyber attacks. In today's digital world, almost everything is connected to the internet, which makes systems vulnerable to hackers and malicious activities.

Cyber attacks can happen in many ways such as malware, phishing, ransomware, and hacking attempts. These attacks can steal sensitive information like passwords, bank details, and personal data.

Cybersecurity professionals use different tools and techniques to detect, prevent, and respond to cyber threats. They monitor networks, test systems for vulnerabilities, and build strong security systems to protect data.

There are different areas of cybersecurity including network security, application security, information security, and digital forensics.
