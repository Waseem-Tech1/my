What is OSINT?

OSINT, or Open Source Intelligence, is the practice of collecting and analyzing publicly available information to support investigations, security assessments, and decision-making. Unlike secretive intelligence gathering, OSINT uses information that is accessible to anyone online, in public records, social media, news, forums, or government databases.

Organizations and cybersecurity professionals use OSINT to identify threats, track digital footprints, and prevent attacks before they happen. Analysts may detect leaked credentials, monitor hacker forums, or gather intelligence about potential targets.

There are many tools and techniques in OSINT, including search engines, social media monitoring, geolocation analysis, and metadata extraction. The goal is to turn raw public information into actionable intelligence while staying legal and ethical.

OSINT is widely used in cybersecurity, law enforcement, corporate security, and competitive intelligence. It helps investigators uncover threats and map digital networks.

In short, OSINT is the art and science of finding intelligence in plain sight.

  • Maltego: Link analysis and data visualization platform.
  • Shodan: Search engine for internet-connected devices.
  • Censys: Platform for discovering internet infrastructure.
  • SpiderFoot: Automated OSINT reconnaissance tool.
  • Amass: Domain enumeration and attack surface mapping.
  • theHarvester: Collects emails, domains, and subdomains.
  • Recon-ng: Modular reconnaissance framework.
  • Have I Been Pwned: Checks if emails were leaked in breaches.
  • OSINT Framework: Directory of OSINT tools.
  • FOCA: Metadata analysis for documents.
  • BuiltWith: Detects technologies used by websites.
  • Social-Searcher: Social media monitoring tool.
  • Metagoofil: Extracts metadata from public documents.
